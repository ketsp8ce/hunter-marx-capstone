Emerging Trends in Computer Science

Part One

What is the significance of each trend?

The two trends I will be examining are Generative AI and 5G networking. Generative AI is the most obvious global trend in not only computer science but technology itself worldwide. AI is infiltrating every area of life, even areas in which its use-case is questionable. 5G, on the other hand, has assumed a quieter but equally transformative takeover. It is faster than any previous networking technology, and its prevalence represents the most significant change in smartphone technology since the first iPhone.

How will each trend change the field of computer science?

Generative AI

Many programmers are now "vibe coding" — relying heavily on AI-assisted code generation. While this technology has broadened what beginners can create, it has also damaged the integrity of technical knowledge. The most drastic negative effect is in security culture and DevSecOps. If team members only partially understand the application’s logic, vulnerabilities are bound to arise.

5G

5G offers higher speeds and lower latency than any internet technology before it. This necessitates new security protocols and drives innovations in edge computing. Its ultra-low latency enables near real-time processing — critical for technologies like autonomous vehicles.

How will each trend change the experience of consumers, workers, or citizens?

Generative AI

Its impact on academia has been immense for both students and teachers. Among average users, AI tools are beginning to replace social interaction. Long-term developmental effects remain unknown, but short-term studies suggest habitual AI use may decrease cognitive performance.

5G

5G allows even remote regions to access high-speed internet, unlocking both opportunities and challenges. However, misinformation has led to EMF conspiracy theories, rooted in misunderstandings of basic physics. Many do not realize that radiation is simply the movement of particles through a medium.

How will each trend fit in with your career interests or aspirations?

Generative AI

Since 2015, I have been fascinated with AI and science fiction concepts. However, I have become disillusioned with the direction generative AI has taken. Rather than enhancing human creativity, it has become a tool for exploitation and superficiality. As a result, I prefer to avoid AI-centric work and focus on other areas of technology.

5G

5G’s advances in speed and reliability have driven innovations in IoT and cloud computing. I am deeply interested in systems engineering and machine code, both of which will benefit from this connectivity revolution.
